# Fame And Money Plan

## Positioning

Core headline:

> My AI flirted for me. Then I fell in love for real.

This should be positioned as a true, tender, Black Mirror-ish story from the
future that already happened.

Do not sell it as a dating bot. That makes it cheap.

Sell it as:

- AI-native memoir.
- Creative coding.
- Terminal cinema.
- Agent culture.
- A human story about automation entering intimacy.

## What We Want

1. GitHub stars and social proof.
2. Press attention from AI, culture, internet, and art publications.
3. A short-film/festival path.
4. Paid speaking or podcast appearances.
5. Sponsors/patrons who want the longer story.
6. Future monetization through film, essay, zine, installation, talk, or agent
   culture brand.

## Monetization Paths

### 1. Sponsor Button

Enable GitHub Sponsors or add a custom funding link. Frame it as:

> Help turn this repo into a short film, essay, and live terminal performance.

### 2. Paid Essay

Write the longform version:

Title options:

- `My AI Flirted For Me. Then I Fell In Love.`
- `The Heart Does Not Have A Stable API`
- `I Automated Hinge And Accidentally Made A Love Story`

Publish on Substack, Medium, or a personal site with paid support.

### 3. Short Film / Festival

Package the existing terminal piece as:

- 3-6 minute festival cut.
- 60 second social cut.
- Artist statement.
- AI-use disclosure.
- Privacy statement.

### 4. Talk

Talk title:

> The Heart Does Not Have A Stable API

Audience:

- AI conferences.
- Creative coding meetups.
- universities.
- internet culture podcasts.
- founder/agent meetups.

### 5. Zine / Digital Artifact

Sell a small PDF/zine:

- story;
- screenshots of terminal art;
- technical architecture;
- emotional postmortem;
- privacy-safe lessons.

### 6. Agent Culture Brand

Use this as the first public artifact of a broader idea:

> AI agents are not just productivity tools. They are becoming emotional
> infrastructure.

## Rules

- Do not expose C.
- Do not publish private chats.
- Do not market this as a pickup product.
- Do not over-explain the tech before people feel the story.
- Lead with the hook, then show the video, then explain the system.
