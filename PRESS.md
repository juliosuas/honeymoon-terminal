# Press Notes

## Project

`honeymoon`

## Logline

A real Black Mirror-ish terminal film about a programmer whose AI agent flirted
for him on Hinge, watched it work, and then fell in love with someone real.

## What Happened

Julio built a social copilot named Jeffrey inside OpenClaw. The agent helped
operate Hinge on a real iPhone: reading profiles, learning preferences, writing
comments, remembering outcomes, and stopping around unsafe or paid actions.

The automation worked. Then one person stopped being a data point. The piece is
about that crossing: when a technical system opens a door into something human.

## Technical Notes

- iPhone-based Hinge automation.
- WebDriverAgent and Appium flows.
- iOS accessibility snapshots.
- Jeffrey/OpenClaw agent orchestration.
- Memory files for preferences, outcomes, corrections, and emotional notes.
- HTML/CSS/JavaScript/SVG browser cut.
- Python/ANSI terminal cut.
- Original chiptune-style sound.

## Core Quote

> My AI flirted for me. Then I fell in love for real.

## Privacy

The public version uses initials and avoids private messages, screenshots,
handles, full names, and identifying details.

## Suggested Angles

- Creative coding as memoir.
- AI agents as emotional infrastructure.
- Dating automation and the limits of optimization.
- GitHub as a place for personal art, not only software.

- A true Black Mirror-style story with a tender ending.
