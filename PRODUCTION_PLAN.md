# Production Plan

Working title: `honeymoon`

Principle: no half measures. The project only moves forward if each version is
more honest, more precise, and more emotionally legible than the last.

## North Star

`honeymoon` is a short film made from code. It must feel like a private love
letter that accidentally invented its own cinema language.

The audience should understand this without explanation:

- J. loved through building.
- C. saw the world inside the code.
- The AI agent was not the lover; it was the bridge.
- The terminal is not a gimmick; it is the emotional material.
- The ending with the cats is not a joke; it is the afterlife of the feeling.

## Non-Negotiables

- No real full names in public cuts.
- No private messages, screenshots, locations, or identifiers that expose C.
- No copyrighted song covers or recognizable melodies without licenses.
- No generic AI visuals that dilute the terminal language.
- No rushed cuts that make the romance feel like content churn.
- No submission until the piece works for a stranger with no context.

## Target Cut

### Festival Cut

- Runtime: 3:00 to 6:00.
- Aspect: 16:9.
- Resolution: 1920x1080 minimum, 4K preferred.
- Frame rate: 24 fps or 30 fps, consistent.
- Audio: original 8-bit/piano hybrid, mixed cleanly.
- Subtitles: burned in or optional track depending on target.
- Language: Spanish primary, with English subtitle version.

### Social Cut

- Runtime: 60 to 90 seconds.
- Aspect: 9:16.
- Hook in first 2 seconds.
- More compression, same dignity.
- Designed for TikTok/Reels, but not shaped by low-effort trend logic.

### Live Terminal Cut

- Runs as code.
- Stable deterministic timing.
- Useful for installation, demo, behind-the-scenes, or artist statement.

## Phase 1 - Lock The Story

Deadline target: 2026-06-24

Deliverables:

- One-page treatment.
- Scene list with exact emotional purpose.
- Final text lines for each chapter.
- Privacy pass.
- Human/AI authorship statement.

Acceptance standard:

- Every scene must answer: why does this need to exist?
- Every text line must be either beautiful, necessary, or removed.
- The story must be understandable without the author explaining it live.

## Phase 2 - Build The Recordable Engine

Deadline target: 2026-07-01

Deliverables:

- `--record-mode` for deterministic timing.
- `--aspect 16:9|9:16`.
- Stable terminal dimensions.
- Configurable chapter durations.
- Export script or documented capture workflow.

Acceptance standard:

- Same command produces repeatable visual timing.
- No text overlap at target aspect ratios.
- Audio and video can be captured cleanly without manual improvisation.

## Phase 3 - Visual Direction Pass

Deadline target: 2026-07-05

Deliverables:

- Improved J. and C. character designs.
- Stronger CDMX empty scene.
- Stronger code-as-courtship scene.
- Slower cat ending with visual restraint.
- Transitional black frames / breath moments.

Acceptance standard:

- The viewer can tell what J. and C. feel by looking at their faces.
- The terminal aesthetic feels intentional, not accidental.
- CDMX empty should feel lonely, not just decorative.

## Phase 4 - Sound And Music

Deadline target: 2026-07-08

Deliverables:

- Original main theme.
- Optional piano-like counterline.
- Final mix for festival cut.
- Audio export independent from live playback.

Acceptance standard:

- No harshness, no accidental clipping.
- Romantic tempo remains intact.
- Sound changes support story beats.
- No copyrighted melodies.

## Phase 5 - First Festival Export

Deadline target: 2026-07-10

Deliverables:

- 16:9 festival screener.
- 9:16 social cut draft.
- 3 stills.
- Poster draft.
- Logline.
- Director statement.
- AI-use disclosure.
- Private screener link.

Acceptance standard:

- A stranger can watch it once and retell the emotional arc.
- The first 15 seconds create curiosity.
- The last 15 seconds land quietly.
- No obvious technical issues: blank frames, bad crop, unreadable text, broken audio.

## Phase 6 - Submission Readiness

Deadline target: 2026-07-12 for Sundance early target

Deliverables:

- Final festival cut.
- Submission metadata.
- English subtitles.
- Spanish and English synopsis.
- Credits.
- Privacy review.

Acceptance standard:

- Ready to submit without apologizing for roughness.
- If rejected, the piece still feels complete and worth sharing.

## Critical Path

1. Treatment.
2. Record mode.
3. Festival cut timing.
4. Visual polish.
5. Audio polish.
6. Export pipeline.
7. Submission packet.

Anything outside this path is secondary until a strong first cut exists.

## Quality Bar

The work is not ready if:

- it needs a long explanation to make sense;
- it feels like a coding demo;
- C. feels like an object instead of a person;
- the AI feels like the protagonist;
- the terminal style makes the story harder to feel;
- the music feels like parody instead of longing;
- the ending feels like a meme instead of a prayer.

The work is ready when:

- someone pauses after the ending;
- the code feels intimate;
- the AI question feels human, not trendy;
- the privacy choices feel elegant;
- the viewer remembers CDMX empty and the cats.
